About

We conducted an analysis comparing and contrasting the performance of
NBC 5, Telemundo Chicago, and its main competitors, ABC 7 & Univision Chicago.

Our evaluation was based on the following criteria:

Website/App Design

Examining the layout and content of each station's website and app, we determined how successful the site was in delivering news through its own online platforms.

Social Media

Comparing each station's social media following and content, we concluded how successful they were in strengthening their brand through Facebook, Twitter, and Instagram.

Survey

In order to receive a better understanding of how these news organizations were viewed by millennials, we sent out a survey to 160 millennials in the Chicago area.

A/B Testing

A/B Testing helps determine the popularity of a product by allowing a sample population to choose between two different versions of a design. Some major news companies have used A/B Testing to increase trafficking on their sites and help editors figure out what would appeal more to their audience.

Survey & Insights



Our survey was filled out by 160 individuals within the age range of 18-40 to understand their media consumption habits. Most respondents were in their early 20s and received some level of college education.

We found that social media was the most popular form of news consumption within this demographic. 90.6 percent of our respondents indicated that they used social media for news.

Only 33.8 percent of the respondents used a news app, and 44 percent said that they were "Somewhat likely" to download an app.

Surprisingly, only 1.9 percent of the respondents said that they preferred to consume news by watching TV. The two most popular forms of news consumption were articles and online videos, favored by 69.2 and 60.4 percent of respondents respectively.

NBC 5 Chicago

  • Website Design

Content

Pros

NBC 5 does an excellent job of covering news within the Chicago area, and it even provides an option to watch live TV that is easy to find. Both the site and app provide thorough articles, accompanied with photos and videos when applicable. Although a photo is not shown for every headline on the site, tags are placed to clearly indicate that an article contains video content. This order makes it easy to read several headlines in rapid succession.

The site also advertises weather, the station’s charitable events, and special features like NBC 5 Responds and Investigates. Additionally, the sports section is very easy to find on the home page.

Cons

Although there are a few tweets in the lower half of the page, the homepage does a poor job of connecting the site to the station’s social media platforms.

Website Design

Desktop

Organization: The top menu of the website is neatly organized and easy to navigate. However, the bottom menu has several options that could confuse the user, and the font is a bit small. Additionally, the home page is a bit confusing because the font size of each category is small and easy to overlook.

Photos: The photos on the homepage could be larger to catch the viewer’s eye.

Feature Size: Many of the buttons on the site, especially those connecting to social media, are small and easy to miss. However, the slideshows and categories have a lightweight and aesthetically pleasing design.

Ad Size & Placement: Although the ads on the homepage are effective, they are large and distracting in other categories of the site. On some pages, the ads take up more space than the actual content. Occasionally, the site opens up ads in another tab, creating an inconvenient experience for the user.

White Space: Placing white space on the sides of a page’s layout is an effective way of concentrating content in one area to focus the user’s eye on what is important. However, NBC 5’s homepage has a little too much white space. The extra space could be used to make photos and videos larger and more eye-catching.

Mobile

Overview: The mobile site stacks headlines, videos, and photos on top of each other to make it easier to scroll through. This simplistic design makes it easy for users to find featured stories on the homepage.

Organization: The menu of the mobile site is not as organized as the desktop version. The side menu reveals several options that the user has to scroll through, which may create a confusing experience. There is also a menu towards the top that the user can slide through, but its functionality is not clear at first. This top menu is also tiny and easy to overlook.

Feature Size: Like the desktop version, the buttons of the mobile site are small and easy to overlook. There are also very few icons that connect to the station’s social media platforms.

Ad Size & Placement: Unlike the desktop version of the site, the ads are placed in a way that they are not overwhelming.

App Design

Overview: The app and mobile site are almost identical. Because of this setup, the two platforms share most of the same pros and cons.

Feature Size: The social media icons are even smaller on the app than they are on the mobile site, making them hard to find.

Push Notifications: The app sends regular and frequent notifications about various topics. Some of the more common topics are about weather, traffic, sports, and breaking news.

Social Media

Facebook: 850 thousand likes & 800 thousand followers

The NBC 5 Facebook page mostly features article links to the station’s website. There appears to be minimal videos and little to no interaction with viewers. These posts do not appear to be very popular, and usually stay within the range of 20-300 likes. When compared to the number of followers on NBC 5’s Facebook page, this ratio is very low.

Twitter: 382 thousand followers

NBC 5’s Twitter page consists of article links and retweets from reporters. The page rarely uses hashtags and does not seem to interact with viewers often. Most Tweets receive very few likes and retweets, only occasionally exceeding 10 likes.

In terms of content, NBC 5’s Twitter page is relatively diverse, focusing on weather, traffic, crime, recalls, and sports.

Instagram: 47.7 thousand followers

NBC 5’s Instagram features images of talent and beautiful shots of Chicago. Many of the photos are re-posts from viewers or reporters, who are encouraged to tag #nbcchicago for a chance to be featured.

The station’s Instagram seems to be more successful than its other social media platforms. Most Instagram posts receive 100-500 likes.

Content

Pros

The content of ABC 7's website correlates well with what is posted on the app. On both the site and the app, ABC 7 delivers thorough news about the Chicago area, along with multimedia content to accompany each story. The multimedia content, which includes videos, photos, and interactive photo slideshows, provides a more engaging experience for the viewer.

ABC 7 localizes its areas of coverage through these platforms, making it easier for viewers to find information that directly applies to them. Its menu is broken down into the following neighborhoods: Chicago & Suburban Cook Co., Northern Suburbs, Western Suburbs, Southern Suburbs, and NW Indiana.

The site and app also have helpful updates about weather on traffic. ABC 7 provides an hourly and weekly weather forecast on both platforms, along with weather maps for various Illinois counties. Additionally, the interactive traffic map creates a more engaging experience for users.

Cons

Although ABC 7 provides extensive coverage, the tone of many of the station's headlines are negative, especially when presenting violent news. For example, on July 20, 2017, the most popular stories were:

Teen fatally shot in Hammond while answering door
3 injured in Edens wreck; more rain falls on flooded communities
2 women arrested after baby falls out of restaurant trash

Although these stories present negative news, these topics can be delivered in a more sensitive manner to account for the communities that are affected by these stories.

Additionally, the menu of both the site and app is too cluttered, overwhelming users with more options than they want. Although the menu of the app is more concise, the website has over 30 options in its dropdown menu alone, making it hard for users to find what they are looking for.

Website Design

Desktop

Organization: The desktop version of the website is cluttered and overwhelming. Although there are several menu sections, it is hard to tell how the stories featured in the homepage relate to these sections.

Photos: The site's design emphasizes that each story has some form of multimedia content to attract the viewer. However, the thumbnails are large and poorly organized in the lower half of the homepage, and they even overshadow the headlines below them. The thumbnails on the sidebar are not even in-line with the other sections of this part of the homepage.

Feature Size: The button size of ABC 7's website are too small, making it easy to miss them. It can be hard to find social media features or identify that something is a slideshow.

Ad Size & Placement: Most of the ads on the site are large and inconvenienty placed. Upon opening the homepage, an ad in the footer appears, which distracts the user from the important content on the site. Although the ads take up a substantial amount of space on the page, the font on some of ABC 7's own ads is too small to read unless the user zooms in.

White Space: ABC 7's site feels cluttered and disorganized because little room is left for white space. Proper spacing not only makes web design more aesthetically pleasing, but also easier for the user to understand the organizational layout.

Mobile

The mobile version of the website is very similar to the desktop version and has much of the same design issues. However, in order to adapt to the smaller mobile frame, the mobile site design is a bit simpler.

App Design

Organization: ABC 7's app is organized more effectively than its website. The stories are easy to find as the user scrolls down the opening page. However, like the website, the app faces the same problem of having too many categories in the main menu.

Feature Size: Like the website, the app's buttons are small and look stylistically outdated.

Push Notifications: The app sends regular and helpful notifications that are mostly about weather. However, this feature requires the user's permission to be enabled.

Social Media

Facebook: 2 million likes and followers

ABC 7 features short previews of its newscasts on its Facebook page, along with beautiful photos of Chicago. Some of the most popular posts are Facebook Live videos, Captured on Camera phone videos from viewers, and questions that ask for a response from followers. These posts receive over 1 thousand likes. Each post also has a caption that starts with an eye-catching introduction in caps.

The ratio of ABC 7's followers to the number of likes on its posts appears to be low. Although ABC 7's Facebook page has close to 2 million followers, the station's posts rarely rarely receive over 1 thousand likes. It is even common for a post to receive as little as 50 likes.

Twitter: 658 thousand followers

The Twitter page mostly features still images from newscasts, with little video content. ABC 7 rarely receives retrweets and likes on its Tweets. Most posts rarely exceed 100 likes and retweets, and many receive as little as 10 likes and retweets.

Instagram: 88.2 thousand followers

Most of ABC 7's Instagram posts receive 200-600 likes. Some of the most popular posts are beautiful photos of Chicago or photos of food. The Instagram features more reposted content from reporters and viewers, encouraging them to tag #abc7chicago.

What is A/B Testing?

A/B Testing is a design process that releases two versions of a product to a sample population in order to determine which would be more successful.

The Boston Globe

The Boston Globe uses A/B Testing on headlines, allowing editors to see which headline would outperform the other. By releasing the same article under two different headlines, the paper can predict which headline would entice more users to read the article. Later, the editor permanently changes the article's headline to the title that was more popular.
